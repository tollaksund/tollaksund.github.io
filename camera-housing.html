Problem Statement: Computer Vision at "Chicken Height"

Developing a robust machine learning algorithm for chicken detection poses a unique set of challenges when the camera is positioned at "chicken height." Unlike top-down surveillance, a ground-level perspective introduces extreme occlusion and dynamic lighting variations.

At this height, individual birds frequently block the view of others, making it difficult for standard object detection models to differentiate between overlapping subjects. To preserve data integrity, the housing must keep the lens pristine while ensuring lighting remains consistent.

Cameras in the poultry house.

Refraction Control

Glass-centered design to reduce direct light interference and internal reflections.

Anti-Peck & Perch

Sloped geometry prevents perching while protecting the internal core from scratching.

Bias Combat

Modular design helps build a robust algorithm by minimizing environmental context bias.

Lower Housing: Access & Motion Compatibility

The bottom part is engineered for a tight, secure fit around the camera base while allowing the lens to look out clearly through the glass container. It features a two-part construction, enabling easy dismantling for maintenance.

Anti-Condensation: Integrated air holes facilitate passive airflow, preventing moisture buildup.

Pan-Tilt Support: Snug fit but provides enough internal clearance for mechanical pan and tilt functions.

Results & Data Collection

The camera housing allowed for capturing images of chickens for a longer period of time.

CAD Design & Engineering

The assembly was modeled in Autodesk Fusion 360 to ensure tight tolerances for 3D printing and precise alignment between the glass optics and the camera lens.
