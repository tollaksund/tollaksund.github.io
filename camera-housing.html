Problem Statement: Why this Housing?

Modern poultry farming relies increasingly on computer vision to monitor bird health and behavior. However, the environment inside a poultry house is one of the most challenging for electronics.

  • High Dust & Dander: Fine particles clog fans and coat lenses within hours.
  • Ammonia Corrosion: Waste creates a corrosive atmosphere that eats through standard metals.
  • Physical Interaction: Chickens are curious; they peck at cables and attempt to perch on any flat surface.

Context: High-dander environment in a typical commercial house.

IP65 Design

Complete protection against dust ingress and low-pressure water jets during cleaning.

Anti-Peck Geometry

Curved surfaces designed to minimize bird interest and prevent perching.

Modular Assembly

Two-part interlocking system for easy lens maintenance and camera calibration.

Upper Housing: Anti-Perch & Seal

The **Top Part** of the housing serves as the primary structural shield. To prevent chickens from perching—which adds weight and obscures the view—I implemented a **high-angle sloped roof geometry**. This ensures birds cannot gain a stable footing.

Engineering Detail: The interface includes a recessed groove for a silicone gasket, ensuring that when the two halves meet, an airtight seal is maintained against the fine dust common in poultry houses.

Lower Housing: Lens & Mounting

The **Bottom Part** is dedicated to optical clarity and internal component mounting. It features a precision-cut acrylic viewport protected by a deep "brow" to prevent shadows and reduce pecking directly at the lens.

  • Integrated PCB standoffs for the camera module.
  • Recessed cable entry point to prevent wire exposure to birds.
  • Heat dissipation venting designed to keep dust out while allowing airflow.
