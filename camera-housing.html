Problem Statement: Computer Vision at "Chicken Height"

Developing a robust machine learning algorithm for chicken detection poses a unique set of challenges when the camera is positioned at "chicken height." Unlike top-down surveillance, a ground-level perspective introduces extreme occlusion and dynamic lighting variations.

At this height, individual birds frequently block the view of others, making it difficult for standard object detection models to differentiate between overlapping subjects. To preserve data integrity, the housing must keep the lens pristine while ensuring lighting remains consistent despite the harsh, high-dander atmosphere.

Glass-centered design to reduce direct light interference and internal reflections.

Anti-Peck & Perch

Sloped geometry prevents perching while protecting the internal core from scratching.

Bias Combat

Modular design helps build a robust algorithm by minimizing environmental context bias.

Upper Housing: Glass Integration & 8-Part Modular Design

The top part is designed around a central glass container, chosen for its optical properties and durability. This structure is specifically built to reduce direct light intake, thereby minimizing internal reflections that could disrupt the detection model. The exterior geometry is steeply sloped to prevent chickens from perching.

8-Part Assembly

Designed in eight separate interlocking parts, allowing for rapid prototyping and easy customization without the high costs associated with large PLA prints.

Optical Shielding

The surrounding segments act as a light shroud, ensuring the glass container only receives filtered light from intended angles.

Lower Housing: Access & Motion Compatibility

The bottom part is engineered for a tight, secure fit around the camera base while allowing the lens to look out clearly through the glass container. It features a two-part construction, enabling the unit to be easily dismantled for maintenance or camera calibration.

Anti-Condensation: Integrated air holes facilitate passive airflow, preventing moisture buildup inside the glass housing.

Pan-Tilt Support: Tolerance levels are carefully balanced; the housing is snug but provides enough internal clearance for the camera's mechanical pan and tilt functions.

Machine Learning Impact: Consistent image quality helps eliminate context bias, ensuring the algorithm identifies birds based on their features rather than environmental artifacts.
